Destination Wedding Planning

About

Planning a destination wedding can be an exciting journey, if given sufficient time and effort. With a variety of destinations and venues to choose from, the options for vendors, and add-ons can be overwhelming. It's important to dedicate time to review all the available options. From invitations, wedding registry, ceremony and reception details, to officiant, flowers, photographer, music, hotel rooms, and much more, destination wedding planning is fulltime commitment. The information provided on this site will guide you through the process and timeline for planning the big event.

Venue

With a large number of locations and venues to choose from, deciding on location is the first step. Once the location is selected, narrowing down the venue options to top 5 and contacting them for information is a helpful approach.

Vendors

Vendor selection for destination weddings focuses primarily on officiant (denominational or non-denominational), photographer, florist, and music. Some venues will have a strict selection of vendors that they work with, and will provide you with 3-5 options to choose from, for each category. Please note that some venues require a wedding planner, and will provide you a selection for that as well.

The Dress

Choosing the wedding dress for the occassion will depend on the venue and theme (if the destination wedding is a beaach wedding, mountain, resort, public area, historical venue. Since destination weddings tend to fall primarily within the beach arena, the designers provided on this site focus on elegant A-line, flowery lace designs, and romantic fitted patterns.

Invitations

To complete the event, sending Save the Date and Wedding Invitations ahead of time, to allow guests plenty of time to research options for flights, hotels, car rental, is highly recommended. A modern approach to RSVP is to include a QR code on the invitation, to cut down on tiem for responses and collect all the responses in a central location. For destination weddings, most vendors will need a confirmation of guest count up to 4-6 months in advance.
